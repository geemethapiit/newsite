Sea Turtles



Turtles are hard-shelled reptiles that shield them from predators. They developed millions of years ago, making them one of the most ancient and primitive groups of reptiles. Turtles may be found living in practically every environment on the planet. The majority of a turtle's life is spent in water. They have webbed feet or flippers, a streamlined body, and are aquatic life-adapted. Except to lay their eggs in the sand, sea turtles hardly ever venture ashore. Freshwater turtles are found in ponds and lakes where they crawl up logs or rocks to enjoy the sun's warmth.

Threats to Turtles



Sea turtle have no natural enemies in the animal kingdom and are an irreplaceable part of marine eco-systems. However, at present, marine turtles are one of the most endangered species in the world, with an 80% decrease in the species in the last 100 years, solely due to human action.


  1. Overfishing & Bycatch

  2. Climate change & Ocean contamination

  3. Ghost Nets & Pollution

  4. Poaching for meat and eggs

  5. Disturbing human activities on beaches
